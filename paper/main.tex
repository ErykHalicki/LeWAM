\begin{abstract}
      World Action Models (WAMs) that jointly predict future video and robot actions have shown strong zero-shot generalization across embodiments and unseen situations~\cite{DreamZero, MimicVideo, UWM}. 
    However, existing WAMs rely on large video diffusion backbones that dedicate much of their capacity to visual details irrelevant to robotic control, requiring server-scale compute that places them out of reach for most researchers and prevents deployment on edge devices.
      We present \textbf{LeWAM}, a compact latent-space World-Action Model that combines a distilled V-JEPA2 video encoder, SmolVLM2, and a flow-matching Diffusion Transformer that jointly predicts future video latents and actions.
      LeWAM is designed to train and run on consumer hardware using open-source, community-collected robot data, testing the limits of world-action modelling at small scale.
  \end{abstract}
