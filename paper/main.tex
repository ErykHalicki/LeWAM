\begin{abstract}
    World Action Models (WAMs) that jointly predict future video and robot actions have recently shown strong zero-shot generalization across robot embodiments and unseen situations\cite{DreamZero}\cite{MimicVideo}.
However, existing WAMs rely on large video diffusion models that require server-scale compute infrastructure which is inaccessible to most researchers, and completely inaccessible to the open-source community.
In an effort to improve open access to robot learning research, we present \textbf{LeWAM}, a compact Latent-Space World-Action Model designed to run on consumer hardware and trained on open-source, community collected robot data.
LeWAM combines a distilled V-JEPA2 video encoder with SmolVLM and a flow-matching Diffusion Transformer (DiT) that jointly predicts future video latents and corresponding actions.

\end{abstract}
